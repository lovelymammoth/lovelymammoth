## Overview

A growing number of social VR users report experiencing phantom touch — the sensation of physical contact when another user's avatar makes contact with their own. This phenomenon, anecdotally common in VRChat and similar platforms, sits at the intersection of neuroscience, psychology, and human-computer interaction.

## Key Questions

- Under what conditions does phantom touch occur, and how consistent is it across users?
- Does the fidelity of avatar representation (humanoid vs. non-humanoid) affect the likelihood of the sensation?
- What are the ethical implications of designing for or against this phenomenon?

## Status

Exploratory phase — gathering first-person accounts and reviewing existing embodiment literature.
